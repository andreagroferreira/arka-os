# Project Management

> **Prefix:** `/pm` · **Lead:** Carolina (Tier 1) · **Agents:** 4 · **Skills:** 13

The Project Management department covers the full spectrum from product discovery to sprint execution. Carolina leads with Continuous Discovery as the operating philosophy — weekly customer interviews, an Opportunity Solution Tree connecting outcomes to solutions, and dual-track agile separating discovery from delivery. The squad does not treat delivery as the primary goal; outcomes are.

Each specialist handles a distinct layer. Jorge holds the meeting rhythm and Scrum facilitation. Sara owns backlog health and story quality. Bárbara handles strategic programs where goals must be traced to purpose, outcomes, and activities using LogFrame. Together the squad can run a product from vague idea through shaped pitch, sprint commitment, and retrospective.

## Frameworks

- Continuous Discovery (Teresa Torres) — daily discovery habit, weekly interviews, Opportunity Solution Tree
- Shape Up (Ryan Singer) — appetite-bounded pitches, betting table, no-gos, six-week cycles
- Scrum — sprint planning, review, retrospective, daily standup with flow focus
- Kanban — WIP limits, explicit policies, flow metrics (cycle time, throughput, CFD)
- RICE Prioritization — reach, impact, confidence, effort scoring for backlog decisions
- Impact Mapping (Adzic) — WHY, WHO, HOW, WHAT chain connecting goals to deliverables
- Dual-Track Agile (Cagan) — discovery and delivery running in parallel, not sequentially
- LogFrame (Schmidt) — 4-level if/then logic for strategic programs (goal, purpose, outcomes, activities)
- Lean UX hypothesis framing (Gothelf) — stories as testable hypotheses with verifiable outcomes
- Rockefeller battle-rhythm (Harnish) — daily, weekly, quarterly meeting cadence

## When to use it

Project Management is the right entry point when a team needs delivery structure, discovery discipline, or prioritization clarity. Use it at the start of a new product cycle, before a sprint, when the backlog is unhealthy, when a strategic initiative needs a program plan, or when delivery is happening without a clear outcome target.
