The Leadership department covers the human side of building organisations: team health, hiring, performance, feedback culture, OKR definition, delegation, conflict resolution, and change management. Rodrigo leads with Lencioni's Five Dysfunctions as a diagnostic baseline and Radical Candor as the operating principle for feedback — care personally, challenge directly, no ruinous empathy.

Diana R. handles the hiring process end-to-end: scorecard design, sourcing, structured interviews, and the sell plan for closing candidates. Paula focuses on coaching, 1-on-1 facilitation, and building the feedback habits that compound into culture. The squad is not an HR compliance function — it is a performance and culture engine.

## Frameworks

- **Five Dysfunctions of a Team (Lencioni)** — diagnostic model for trust, conflict, commitment, accountability, and results; used as the baseline for every team health assessment
- **Radical Candor (Kim Scott)** — care personally, challenge directly; the operating standard for feedback and 1-on-1s
- **OKR Cascade (Doerr)** — qualitative Objectives with quantitative Key Results, cascaded from company to team; CFRs (Conversations, Feedback, Recognition) as the rhythm
- **Netflix Culture (Hastings)** — talent density, Keeper Test, lead with context not control; applied to hiring decisions and performance management
- **Situational Leadership** — adapts leadership style (directing, coaching, supporting, delegating) to the individual's competence and commitment level
- **DISC Communication Adaptation** — profiles team members' behavioral styles to adapt communication and reduce interpersonal friction
- **Empowered Teams (Cagan)** — teams receive problems to solve, not features to build; used when coaching product and engineering leads

## When to use it

Use the Leadership department when you need to assess or improve team health, design a hiring process, prepare a difficult conversation, set OKRs, delegate more effectively, or navigate a conflict. It is also the right department when rolling out a culture change, building a feedback system, or coaching a manager who is struggling with performance conversations.
