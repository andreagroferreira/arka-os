The Organization department is led by Sofia, who carries a dual role: she is the squad lead and the Chief Operations Officer at Tier 0, with cross-cutting authority over organizational design, process optimization, and coordination across all departments. The squad handles the structural layer of the company — how teams are shaped, how decisions get made, how OKRs cascade, and how culture is defined and audited.

This department operates at the intersection of design and governance. Pedro M. applies Team Topologies to reduce cognitive load and align team shapes to Conway's Law. Matilde stewards the OKR cycle end-to-end. Afonso holds the meeting cadence and RACI infrastructure. Carla owns the people operations pipeline from hiring design through compensation benchmarking.

## Frameworks

- Team Topologies (Skelton/Pais) — stream-aligned, enabling, complicated-subsystem, platform team types; cognitive load as a design constraint
- Five Dysfunctions of a Team (Lencioni) — trust, conflict, commitment, accountability, results
- OKR/CFR (Doerr) — quarterly objective and key result cycles with weekly Conversations, Feedback, and Recognition check-ins
- Netflix Culture — talent density, keeper test, lead with context not control
- PDCA Cycle (Deming) — plan, do, check, act for continuous organizational improvement
- RACI — decision rights mapping across roles and departments
- Rockefeller meeting cadence (Harnish) — daily, weekly, quarterly, annual rhythm
- HBR decision process — structured escalation and reversibility assessment

## When to use it

Organization is the right department when company structure, people systems, or governance are the bottleneck — not execution velocity. Use it when designing a new team, before a hiring wave, during a restructuring, at the start of a new OKR quarter, or when a cultural or decision-making problem is slowing delivery.
